# Service Level Agreement (SLA)

## Uptime Guarantees

### SLA Tiers by Plan

**Starter Plan**
- 99.5% uptime guarantee
- Monthly uptime: 99.5% (≈3.6 hours downtime/month)
- Response time: 24 business hours

**Professional Plan**
- 99.9% uptime guarantee
- Monthly uptime: 99.9% (≈43 minutes downtime/month)
- Response time: 4 business hours

**Enterprise Plan**
- 99.99% uptime guarantee
- Monthly uptime: 99.99% (≈4 minutes downtime/month)
- Response time: 1 hour
- Dedicated infrastructure & redundancy

## Incident Response Times

### Priority Levels

**P0 - Critical**
- Initial Response: 1 hour
- Target Resolution: 4 hours
- Impact: Complete service unavailable

**P1 - High**
- Initial Response: 2 hours
- Target Resolution: 8 hours
- Impact: Core functionality degraded

**P2 - Medium**
- Initial Response: 4 hours
- Target Resolution: 24 hours
- Impact: Non-critical features affected

**P3 - Low**
- Initial Response: 24 hours
- Target Resolution: 5 business days
- Impact: Cosmetic issues, feature requests

## Service Credits

### Credit Calculation

If monthly uptime falls below SLA:
- 99.0-99.49% downtime: 5% credit
- 98.0-98.99% downtime: 10% credit
- Below 98.0% downtime: 25% credit

Credits applied to next month's invoice automatically.

### Exclusions

SLA does not apply to:
- Scheduled maintenance windows (announced 72 hours in advance)
- Customer-side issues (network, firewall, etc.)
- DDoS attacks or security breaches
- Third-party service failures

## Root Cause Analysis (RCA)

**For any P0 incident lasting >1 hour:**
- RCA report required within 24 hours
- Published on customer dashboard
- Includes timeline, root cause, remediation steps, prevention measures

## Escalation Matrix

- Technical support unresolved after 48 hours → escalate to engineering
- Unresolved critical issue after 72 hours → escalate to VP Engineering
- Breached SLA → automatic credit + executive review
